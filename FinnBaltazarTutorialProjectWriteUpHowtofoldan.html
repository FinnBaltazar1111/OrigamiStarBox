You’ll need a large piece of square paper. I don’t recommend trimming a non-square piece of paper; you need to be precise. Make sure to be as precise as possible; take your time with each fold. Crease all folds.

1.) First, fold your paper in half on all major axes. This means in half both ways diagonally and vertically & horizontally.

2.) Re-fold your paper diagonally. There should already be a crease. Push each side of the new triangle up and into the inside of the diagonal crease so that the two triangles that used to be on the edges are hidden.

3.) Rotate the paper until the fully open side is facing away from you.         Then fold the right and left edges of the first layer as far down and toward the middle crease as possible while keeping the top edge flat on the table. Unfold.

4.) Squash the same edges toward the creases you just made. Then tuck the outer sections of the new squash crease into the space between the two layers.

5.) Repeat steps 3-4 on the back side.

6.) Rotate the paper upside down. Next, fold the edge facing you as far away from you as possible without lifting any other flaps.

7.) Repeat step 6 on all other layers/sides.

8.) Finally, lift the two flaps on opposite sides to a 90-degree angle (so together, they form a 180-degree angle), and pull them away from each other gently.
